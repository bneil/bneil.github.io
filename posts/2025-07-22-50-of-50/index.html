50 Blog Posts in 50 Days (ish)

Posted on Jul 20, 2025
article is 910 words and should take 5 minutes to consume 👀

The Challenge That Is Getting Away From Me. Lol.

I stumbled into this whole thing through some RSS rabbit hole that led me to blogroll.org, then to forking mad’s completed challenge, and eventually to Lou Plummer’s 50 Ideas for Blog Posts. The prompts looked fun, the challenge seemed doable, and I figured, “Why not throw my hat in the ring?”

Well, turns out maintaining daily consistency while juggling everything else is… challenging. Who knew? But you know what? I’m still plugging away at it, and honestly, some of my favorite posts have come out of this whole experiment.

The Complete 50of50 Collection

Here’s every post I’ve written as part of this challenge so far. Some are deep dives, some are quick thoughts, and some are just me rambling about whatever the prompt made me think about. That’s kind of the point, right?

  • day01-of-50-blog-posts - Day 1, A road trip you've taken that was memorable
  • day02-of-50-blog-posts - Day 2, On what subjects are you outspoken and on what subjects are you mostly mute?
  • day03-of-50-blog-posts - Day 3, Why do you like your favorite restaurant?
  • day04-of-50-blog-posts - Day 4, What woman in your family do you most admire and why?
  • day05-of-50-blog-posts - Day 5, What is the most memorable job you've held?
  • day06-of-50-blog-posts - Day 6, What is or was your relationship with people from your parent's generation?
  • day07-of-50-blog-posts - Day 7, Do you have any unpopular opinions or dislike something that many people like?
  • day08-of-50-blog-posts - Day 8, What is the funniest book or TV show with which you are familiar?
  • day09-of-50-blog-posts - Day 9, Do you have a gratitude practice? Why or why not? What sort of things are you grateful for?
  • day10-of-50-blog-posts - Day 10, What's your relationship with physical labor? Do you enjoy, accept or avoid it?
  • day11-of-50-blog-posts - What is your current relationship with technology? Do you enjoy it? Does it cause you anxiety?
  • day12-of-50-blog-posts - Day 12, What hobbies have you had in the past that you no longer have? What happened?
  • day13-of-50-blog-posts - Day 13, What societal problems is your region known for and do they affect you?
  • day14-of-50-blog-posts - Day 14, What has your relationship with music been like throughout your life?

  • day16-of-50-blog-posts - Day 16, What things are easy for you to write about? What things are hard?
  • day17-of-50-blog-posts - Day 17, What are your favorite books and why?
  • day18-of-50-blog-posts - Day 18, How do you feel about alcohol
  • day19-of-50-blog-posts - Day 19, What is your favorite food? Why do you like it? What is your history with it?
  • day20-of-50-blog-posts - Day 20, How do you like to watch movies? Are you a theater goer, a streamer, or do you like physical media?
  • day21-50-blog-posts - Day 21, What kind of adventures have you had pursuing your hobbies?

  • day29-of-50-blog-posts - Day 29, Can you make a list of 20 moments in your current or past life that you would classify as sublime?

  • day37-of-50-blog-posts - Day 37, Do you have any good or bad stories from your relationship with social media or Internet communities?
  • day38-of-50-blog-posts - Day 38, How do you feel about writing? Is it often cathartic, fun, a chore?

What I’ve Learned So Far

Honestly? Writing regularly is harder than I thought it would be, but also more rewarding. Some days the prompts spark something interesting, other days I’m just trying to get words on the page. But that’s kind of the beauty of it - not every post has to be profound. Sometimes it’s just nice to capture a moment or a thought.

Take a look through 50of50 tag which has become my little breadcrumb trail for this experiment. It’s been fun to look back and see the variety of topics and moods. Road trips, personal philosophies, random observations - it’s all there.

If you’re thinking about doing something similar, my advice? Don’t worry too much about the timeline. The writing is the point, not the schedule.
