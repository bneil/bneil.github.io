## B is for Brainstorming

> Have you got "hopes, dreams, and goals" for your blog? If not, why are you doing this?

Im doing this because its a challenge. The warning from this post was that writing for writing
sake can cause burnout, but im not sure. Not writing causes burnout as well. Those thoughts and
words never find there way to a medium and end up never being made. Thats a pretty sad fate. And
a good reason to write. Self expression and all that.

That said, I wouldn't mind taking more stabs at writing some more reviews on some books ive recently
read. For instance, 'A short stay in hell', it was only a two hour audio book, but its one of the
best expressions of hell ive ever read. The writing so tight and well done that ive listened to it twice
and will probably end up buying the book. So good, and id love to break it down a bit more. Of course,
ive been using a lot of libraries and frameworks lately, to help on the job. Even finished up an AI course.

LLMs are controversial, especially in the communities I love. But take yesterday, I watched as the following
happened:

1. Claude code accidentally leaked its source map to npm
2. People downloaded the src.zip
3. Hackers created npm packages based off the names of the private packages named in the src.zip
4. People accidentally using either LLMs to recreate the source map, use those public packages
5. Supply chain attack

I've never seen anything like it. Like the axios leak, I could see. That was a pretty neat compromise. But
sometimes the speed of these things are very intriguing. But this is all super niche, so I could just do some
writeups about tv like Invincible or Survivor 50.

heh, I don't know, but appreciate the brainstorming session.
