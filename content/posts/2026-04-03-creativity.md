## C is for creativity

I have been stealing the ideas for the daily prompts based off the categories provided
with the A to Z challenge main blog. Which hasn't been creative. Going to stop doing that
going forward but its been nice to have a break in thinking up words to write about.

So in the spirit of creativity, I picked a random 'C' word. I present 'concession'.

When I think of concession, its more of that classic scene where two political figures are both giving
in a little to find a consensus on a bill. But that also comes into play in business where its important
to let things go, just find a path.  But more personally, concessions are something you take when trying
to find your own path forward.

For me, when I was younger, I really wanted to be a writer. But when I finished High School, I didn't know
what I should do. So since I had the privilege of working on computers and really enjoyed programming, I thought
that a life as an coder could be as inspired as one of a writer. I told myself that they shared alot of the same
benefits. Ideally working on creative projects, getting to listen to music and generally always being a field
that would provide mental challenges. So I compromised, I conceded, I gave up a dream of becoming a writer and
instead became a programmer.

It's been a really fun career. Met a lot of really wonderful people and worked on interesting projects. Even today
im still running into new and exciting problems that take some real thinking to work out. And staying 'cutting edge'
with whats available has also been a challenge. I've gone from hand crafting code to basically using a mixture of
LLMs to do what I previously had worked on for almost twenty years. When working on personal projects, I still enjoy
taking my time and writing all my code. Working out the system and api designs rather then using an LLM. Just because
it keeps me sharp.

But what i trying to say is that I made a concession to my dreams that I now regret, often thinking about the stories
I've yet to write. However, I know I still have time, and perhaps I'll finally start putting some of them on paper.
